Texas high school golf club marched out of practice session after spotting scantily-clad STRIPPERS

Publish date: 2024-02-19

Tee off or t-shirt off? 

Vista Ridge High School's golf team had to leave practice early and phone their parents to pick them up at Avery Ranch golf course in Austin, Texas, after they spotted scantily-clad strippers enjoying the holes on Monday. 

In a letter sent home to parents on Tuesday, obtained by Fox 7, the school district admitted the teens got an eyeful when they showed up to practice and saw adults acting 'very inappropriately.' 

'Unfortunately, our golfers did witness some lewd behavior,' the letter reportedly read. 'The coach immediately canceled practice and had all players contact their parents to come pick them up.

'What occurred yesterday is not acceptable and we will work closely with Avery Ranch to ensure this never happens again.' 

Photos shared by a local journalist showed two women in crop tops and thongs strutting around the course, with their unusual attire outraging Vista Ridge staff.

Yellow Rose partner Jonathan Joseph told DailyMail.com on Thursday that the company rents the 'entire course for the whole day' and have done the tournament for the past four years and is only adults are allowed to attend. 

'The high school golfers were only on the driving range not the actual course,' Joseph told DailyMail.com. 'This is an area our marshals did not and were not supposed to supervise. We were completely unaware that The golf course scheduled a high school practice at the same time as the Yellow Rose Tournament.' 

Joseph also said the golf course's narrative that they did not know the nature of the tournament and to 'fein surprise' was 'disingenuous.' 

'This was their mistake,' Joseph said. 'We have not received any reports of "lewd behavior" from either the course, the course marshals, or guests of The Yellow Rose. 

'The two young ladies in the blacked out picture produced by Fox News were wearing string bikini bottoms; no different than you would see at Lake Travis on a summer weekend. If somebody saw something they deemed offensive The Yellow Rose sincerely apologizes. This was supposed to be a private event.' 

Vista Ridge's golf team has practiced at the ranch for two decades and was said to have resumed practice on Tuesday. 

The school is working closely with the ranch to make sure it doesn't happen again.
