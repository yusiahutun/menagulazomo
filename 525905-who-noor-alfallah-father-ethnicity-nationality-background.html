Who is Noor Alfallah?

Noor was born on the 2nd of December 1993 in Kuwait. She is a product of the University of Southern California's Cinematic School of Arts. She also has a master's degree in film and production from the University of California Los Angeles.

Who are Noor Alfallah's parents?

Noor grew up around Beverly Hills, California. Her father, Falah N Al-Falah, is the president of Thirty-One Capital Inc., stationed in Beverly Hills, California. He has been in the business for over 14 years, and his company averages an annual turnover of $421,111.

Falah also worked as an investment advisor for Unicorn Investment Bank based in Kuwait, making over $1,512,557 over three years. Therefore, Noor Alfallah's parents' net worth is over $3 million.

Noor also has two sisters, Sophia and Remi, who are also in the entertainment industry, and a brother named Nasser Falah.

Career

Noor embarked on her journey in the entertainment and film industry at a young age as an assistant producer. Her debut film was Streets of Life, where she was an assistant director.

She further elevated her status by producing two short films, La Perir Morit and Brosa Nostra, which debuted in 2018 and 2019. Brosa Nostra's plot revolves around a fraternity president trying to get his house to return to Greek Row at Southern California College.

The film producer has steadily built herself in filmmaking, showcasing her versatility in producing two other films, Billy Knight and Little Death. Billy Knight stars actors Pacino, Patrick Schwarzenegger, and Diana Silvers.

Noor also operates her own production company and is the vice president of Lynda Obst Production at Sony and a film producer. The production firm has the film How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days under its title.
