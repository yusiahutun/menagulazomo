Naming a child is something precious that many parents and guardians spend an extraordinary amount of time to get right. When it comes to the Kisii community, you can be amazed at what names parents are willing to gift their male children. All Kisii baby boy names have an interesting meaning.

The boy child is always assumed to be macho and strong. This has made their feelings to be sidelined, forgetting that they also have a sensitive side that is out rightly ignored. Without appropriate attention, a male child can turn out to be less than average due to the lack of proper naming. This is one of the reasons why Kisii boy names have the oomph to make the baby boy become a proud grown man in his future days. Naming a Kisii baby boy is not just about tradition but about his future.

Intriguing Kisii names and meanings

The Kisii language is quite intriguing and rhythmical. It catches the attention of both locals and foreigners that may not have the slightest clue of the meaning of words. The following baby boy names may have eerie implications but are bound to get your attention:

  • Gesimba – In the Kisii community, it is common to name your baby boy child as an animal. This is why the name Gesimba is quite common in Kisii land. It means mongoose.
  • Ong’iti – This is a preferred name for males, it means snake.
  • Moochi - Just in case you thinking you have heard the weirdest Kisii boy name, then think again. This name means madman.
  • Masongo – This is not only a unique name but one that leaves you with a question mark. It means mucus and works for both boys and girls.
  • Ombui – This is a name that can make or break your child. It can either make them more robust from the bullying they get from school or break them into pieces. This boy name means anus.
  • Ngoge – The name means a warthog.
