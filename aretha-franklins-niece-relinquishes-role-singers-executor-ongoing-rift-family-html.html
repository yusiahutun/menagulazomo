Aretha Franklin's niece relinquishes role as singer's executor due to ongoing 'rift in the family'

Publish date: 2024-04-04

Aretha Franklin passed away in August of 2018 without a known will for her family to work with.

And the Queen of Soul's niece Sabrina Owens put the fate of the late singer's estate back into limbo, as she stepped down from her position as executor Thursday.

Owens, who was appointed by Franklin's four sons, cited that her departure comes as the result of a family rift stemming from the discovery of handwritten wills last year.

Estate: Aretha Franklin's niece stepped down as executor of the Queen of Soul's estate due to handwritten wills being uncovered in the late singer's home last year. The star is seen in 2012 above

Stepping down: Sabrina Owens (pictured here) filed a lengthy letter to the suburban Detroit court detailing the reasons behind relinquishing her role as state executor; pictured here at Franklin's funeral service in August of 2018

In a letter filed Thursday in a suburban Detroit court, Owens wrote, 'given my aunt's love of family and desire for privacy, this is not what she would have wanted for us, nor is it what I want.'  

'I hope that my departure will allow the business of the estate to continue, calm the rift in my family and allow me to return to my personal life.'

Owens works as an administrator at the University of Michigan.

Nine months after Franklin's sons appointed Owens, the handwritten wills were discovered in the late singer's home, including one under cushions in the living room.

 Contents: One document seemed to indicate that Franklin wanted a son, Kecalf Franklin, to serve as executor or representative of the estate; Franklin photographed attending the Mica and Ahmet Ertegun Atrium at Jazz at Lincoln Center in 2015

One document seemed to indicate that Franklin wanted a son, Kecalf Franklin, to serve as executor or representative. 

Sabrina explained that she only became manager of the estate at the request of Franklin's four sons.

Upon the wills being uncovered, a judge last August said a handwriting expert could examine the documents to determine the validity.

Big money: The estate reported assets of $17 million at the end of August, including master recordings and publishing rights worth an estimated $10.5 million; Franklin photographed performing on Late Night with Seth Meyers in December of 2015

Another son, Theodore White II, asked the judge to make him co-representative of the estate with Owens, as a result of the contents of the handwritten documents. 

In terms of stepping down, Owens made clear that it is not being done out of ill will or hurt feelings.

'I love my cousins, hold no animosity towards them, and wish them the best.' 

Icon: Aretha Franklin performed at the 40th Annual Grammy Awards in New York in 1998

Meanwhile, court filings show a mediator has been privately working with the parties.  

Owens' 14-day resignation notice was reported by the Detroit Free Press and a court hearing that was scheduled before her decision is set for Tuesday.

The estate reported assets of $17 million at the end of August, including master recordings and publishing rights worth an estimated $10.5 million. 

The Internal Revenue Service in October filed tax claims of more than $6 million.
