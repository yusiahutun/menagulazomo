Kenyans Celebrate Drop In KPLC Token Prices on Strong Shilling: &quot;Hope It Goes Down More&quot;

Publish date: 2024-03-15
  • Kenya shilling continued to record strong performance against major international currencies, including the US dollar
  • Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) reduced electricity token prices due to ease in foreign exchange fluctuation rate
  • KPLC customers expressed their happiness over a drop in prices of electricity, some hoping for a further decrease

TUKO.co.ke journalist Wycliffe Musalia brings over five years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting, offering deep insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

Kenyans continue to feel an ease in the cost of living as the value of the shilling gains against the US dollar.

Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) customers shared their experience with a drop in electricity token prices.

What's the current KPLC token price?

Between February 2024 and April 2024, the token amount increased by over 2 kilowatts per unit, depending on the tariff.

Customers purchasing tokens at KSh 500 received 16.09 units in April 2024 compared to 14.99 units in February 2024.

For KSh 5,000 spent in March 2024 on tokens, KPLC allocated 150.47 units compared to 160.94 units in April 2024.

What Kenyans said about drop in KPLC token prices

Most Kenyans who purchased electricity tokens within the same period welcomed the decrease in prices, some hoping for more.

"Looks like KPLC have relaxed the price of electricity a bit. For the same amount, I have got 10 units more! Ama ni bonus leo? Whatever the case, I appreciate," a KPLC customer shared on Facebook.

Pst Mike Mukasa replied:

"I'm hopeful. I think the worst is behind us...."

Karuri SK said:

"I got 23 units for KSh 500 yesterday from 17 units for the same amount a month ago. Meaning 230 units for KSh 5,000."

Muriungi Gikundi argued:

"The prices have gone down by 20%."

Kimani G-joh noted:

"I have realised that too. I hope the cost goes down more."

The move by the electricity distributor to lower token prices could be linked to the appreciation of the Kenya shilling against the greenback.

Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) data showed the shilling traded below the KSh 130 mark per dollar.

This means the utility firm's foreign exchange fluctuation rate has been easing since February when the shilling started to strengthen against the US dollar.

How is shilling strength affecting KPLC token prices?

In January 2024, the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) raised electricity prices against the backdrop of declining shilling.

Kenya Power token prices shot up by 16.5% as the shilling fell to KSh 160 against the US dollar.

This is after the regulator hiked the foreign exchange rate fluctuation adjustment charge by 103.7% to a high of KSh 6.46 per unit, up from KSh 3.17.
