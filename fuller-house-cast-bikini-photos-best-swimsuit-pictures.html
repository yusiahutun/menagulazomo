The Cast of Fuller House Has Mastered the Art of Poolside Selfies! See Their Best Bikini Photo

Publish date: 2024-05-13

The cast of Fuller House is full of fashionistas! The Netflix sitcom led by Candace Cameron Bure, Jodie Sweetin and Andrea Barber ran from 2016 to 2020. And while the Tanner clan is no longer gracing our television screens with their incredible acting skills, they continue to post their best style moments and swimsuit photos on social media. 

Full House became extremely popular during its initial run on ABC from 1987 to 1994. The show’s fanbase grew even more with its syndications to other networks. The sitcom’s leading ladies gained critical acclaim with their incredible acting skills and camaraderie throughout all eight seasons. 

Though Mary-Kate Olsen and Ashley Olsen did not return to Fuller House to reprise their role of Michelle Tanner, they have gone on to launch extremely successful careers in the fashion industry. The twins created their own fashion label called The Row in 2006. The company has since expanded from women’s clothing and accessories to menswear and children’s clothes. 

Mary-Kate and Ashley were not the only stars of Full House who have ventured into the world of fashion. Candace also started her own fashion and lifestyle brand after rising to fame. Her biggest supporters are her husband, Valeri Bure, and their three children, Natasha, Maksim and Lev. 

The Hallmark star and her hubby celebrated their 26th wedding anniversary by going to Florida with their sons in June 2022. Candace shared several breathtaking bikini photos and beachside selfies to mark the occasion. The Dancing With the Stars alum later posted a video of her beauty routine, giving fans all the scoop on how she keeps her skin looking so radiant. Candace takes style and beauty inspiration from some of Hollywood’s most fashionable stars, both past and present. 

“There are so many wonderful style icons like Audrey Hepburn and Marilyn Monroe,” the former cohost of The View told Us Weekly in December 2017. “I love watching Zendaya or Victoria Beckham hit the red carpet, but my real style icons are the women that I see walking around every day. They’re really who I pick up a lot of my fashion cues from. It’s not about a stylist dressing them, or not necessarily wearing what’s most trendy or fashionable, but [it’s about] them feeling confident and good about themselves.”
