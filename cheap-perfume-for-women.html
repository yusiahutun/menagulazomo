How To Shop For An Inexpensive Fragrance

Don't Get Hung Up on Labels: Just because a perfume doesn't carry a designer label, doesn't mean it isn't good. Don't get too caught up with specific brands and instead think about what notes you like best.

Travel Size: If you're really loving a designer perfume, consider purchasing the smallest size available. Rollerballs and solid perfumes are more affordable, and they're less of a long-term commitment.

Think About the Retailer: If you're at a high-end department store, chances are you're going to be around more expensive fragrance options. Check out more mass retailers, like Amazon, CVS, and Target—you'll be surprised at what perfume gems you might dig up.
