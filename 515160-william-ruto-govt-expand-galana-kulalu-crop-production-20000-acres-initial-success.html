William Ruto Says Gov't to Expand Galana-Kulalu Crop Production to 20k Acres after Initial Success

Publish date: 2024-10-06
  • President William Ruto said the initial crop production trial in the Galana-Kulala irrigation scheme proved successful
  • The head of state said the government would partner with the private sector to improve food production
  • The multi-billion shilling irrigation scheme in Galana Kulalu is being activated, albeit in phases, with the government targeting to irrigate over 200,000 acres

President William Ruto has promised to expand the Galana-Kulala irrigation scheme to 20,000 acres in 2023 and subsequently to 200,000 acres within four years.

The head of state said food production would be increased after the initial 500-acre trial was successful.

In a statement on his Twitter account, Ruto said the government plans to eliminate hunger during his first term in office.

"The first 500-acre trial crop in Galana. Great success. Next year, with the private sector, we will expand to 20,000 acres and progressively to 200,000 acres in four years. This is part of our food security plan to eliminate the shame of hunger from our motherland. Forward always," he tweeted.

Galana model farm takes shape

The multi-billion shilling irrigation scheme in Galana Kulalu is being activated, albeit in phases, as evidenced by the photos from the site during a visit by government officials.

Head of Public Service Felix Koskei, on Thursday, July 6, led the relevant government bureaucrats on a tour of the vast project where they assessed its implementation.

A 10,000-acre tract is ready food for production with maize being the main crop; a total of 20,000 acres is to be covered with maize.

To undertake the project on the parcel, Twiga Foods, a private investor partnered with the National Irrigation Board (NIB) under the Public Private Partnership (PPP) regulatory framework.

Twiga Foods' convinced the government with its elaborate Project Implementation Proposal (PIP) as regards food production on a 10,000-acre model farm.

William Ruto revokes Galana subdivision

In January, President William Ruto made an extensive tour of the Galana-Kulalu project.

After the tour, the president revoked the indented subdivision of the project into settlement schemes.

The president further said the government would construct a dam to bring another 350,000 acres under food production.
