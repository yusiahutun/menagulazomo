David Copperfield sets record paying $17.5million for Las Vegas home

Publish date: 2024-06-02

Illusionist David Copperfield has managed to conjure up his perfect home.

The magician, who headlines at MGM Grand on the Strip, paid $17.55million in late June for a northwest Las Vegas house, setting a record for real estate in the city.

The price eclipsed the $17.375 million paid in 2007 for a mansion at the Shadow Creek Golf Club in North Las Vegas, said The Greater Las Vegas Association of Realtors said Thursday.

Copperfield, known for outrageous stunts such as escaping from Alcatraz and making the Statue of Liberty 'disappear,' bought the four-story 31,000-square-foot home eight bedroom home in late June, according to the Review Journal.

Illusionist David Copperfield, who is reportedly worth $800million, put a tiny chink in that fortune by paying out an area-record of $17.55million for a home in Summerlin 

Ivan Sher, Copperfield and fiance Chloe Gosselin's real estate broker, said the glamorous couple, who have a daughter, had been looking for years for a home in the area but hadn't found one that 'spoke' to them

The gated nearly 1.6-acre property at 1625 Enclave Court in Summerlin also features a full-service spa, nightclub, golf simulation room, Zen garden, resort-style infinity swimming pool, two elevators, a gym, wine cellar, movie theater, 11.5 bathrooms, and guest quarters.

The seller, health care executive Kevin Hooks, bought the land for $2.575 million in 2005 and built the house around 2011. It was designed by Swaback Partners of Scottsdale, Arizona.

The house was reportedly not on the market when Copperfield took a liking to it, according to the outlet, but their broker had heard the seller might be amenable, so he approached the owner.

The sprawling 31-thousand square foot home with guest quarters is in one of Las Vegas' most exclusive gated communities, Enclave Court in Summerlin (above)

Ivan Sher, who brokered the deal for the Shapiro & Sher Group, the luxury division of Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices, said the deal closed a few weeks after Copperfield and his fiancée, Chloe Gosselin, caught sight of it and fell in love.

'It’s in an enclave with some of the most impressive homes in all of Las Vegas,' Sher told the outlet. 'A lot of people who sell luxury houses don’t know about this little neighborhood. It’s sensational and David and Chloe felt an instant connection with the home’s style and its surroundings.'

'David and Chloe had been looking for years,' Sher told the Wall Street Journal. 'Nothing really spoke to them.'

Despite the record-setting price, it shouldn't put a dent in Copperfield's bank account, The 59-year-old illusionist is worth $800million, according to Forbes.

The average median home price in Las Vegas is $229,250. At least one real estate expert doesn't think that Copperfield's record price will affect prices in the lower market - nor is it that much money, considering.

'Given the amenities involved, it’s a reasonable price,'  Mark Stark, Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices Nevada Properties CEO told the Review Journal.

The same property would have been more expensive in San Francisco or New York, noted Forrest Barbee, a broker with Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices California Properties.

Copperfield is no stranger to real estate investments, he already owns a four-story Manhattan penthouse, the world’s largest museum of magic history, and Musha Cay at Copperfield Bay, an archipelago of 11 islands in the Bahamas, according to the outlet.
