Woman Asked to Dress Appropriately by Male Bosses Opens Own Law Firm, Wins over Ex-Employers' Client

Publish date: 2024-04-10
  • Kathleen Martinez is an immigration lawyer based in Dallas and handles cases of immigrants seeking to gain legal status
  • She disclosed that her former bosses had insisted on her dressing conservatively for her to be taken seriously by clients
  • Kathleen started her own pink themes law firm and said she took all her former employer's clients

A female attorney who had been told by her male bosses to dress appropriately has opened her own law firm.

Stole their clients

The Immigration law practitioner identified as Kathleen Martinez said she had been told better dressing would make clients take her more seriously

To add insult to injury, Kathleen 'stole' all her former employer's clients whom she now represents.

According to the New York Post, the attorney who is based in Dallas, Texas, runs the Martinez Immigration firm, which helps to assist immigrants to gain legal status.

Rejection by employer

Elsewhere, a differently abled woman started her own company after facing rejection by her employer.

Maitri Shah opted to start her own training and recruitment company now boasts 30 placements of disabled people in various organisations.

Shah has been in a wheelchair for as long as she can remember but was never bothered by it.

She passed well in her academics and would never let pity parties get the better of her.

"I’d have doctor appointments, but after, my parents would take me to the beach and we’d make a day out of it. I’d tell my friends that I’m going out on a picnic; I didn’t want anyone to pity me," she said.
