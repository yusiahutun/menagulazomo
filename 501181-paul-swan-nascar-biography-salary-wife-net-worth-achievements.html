Publish date: 2024-09-17

When Paul Swan was young, he had no ambitions of becoming a rally or race car driver. He always wanted to participate in another sport, including football. So, car racing was something Paul stumbled upon accidentally after playing college football. And when the opportunity presented itself well, he became a strength trainer at NASCAR. His involvement with NASCAR also made him become a reality TV series cast member, propelling his career further.

Paul Swan is a professional rally driver, strength trainer at NASCAR, and front tire carrier. He is well known within the NASCAR community as the front tire carrier for the NASCAR cup series 3 team.

He is a cast member in the reality TV series Austin Dillon’s Life in the Fast Lane, where he is the hype man on the show. Before NASCAR, Paul played college football and was the captain of his football team, The Bowling Green Falcons.

Paul Swan's biography

The driver was born on 23 August 1990 (age 33 as of 2023) in Wisconsin, United States. He is the son of Scott Swan (father) and Meribeth Swan (mother). Also, he has one sibling whose name is not in the public domain.

Paul Swan grew up in Wisconsin. He attended Oconomowoc High School, a public high school in Oconomowoc, Wisconsin, United States. After high school, he attended Bowling Green State University on a scholarship to study Exercise Science.

He subsequently played football for his college team, The Bowling Green Falcons, as a linebacker and became a two-year captain of his team until his career ended in 2013. But after playing college football for four years, he moved to North Carolina, United States, where he developed a passion for NASCAR.

Professional career

Paul did not know much about NASCAR after playing college football. He drove all over the Midwest trying to build a career as a racer. Paul participated in model and ARCA races while taking all the opportunities to train himself.

He then became a pit crew member at NASCAR. Paul joined RCR in 2014 as a strength trainer for the team. However, he rose through the ranks and became the team’s tire carrier. In October 2022, he signed a 6-year contract with Richard Childress Racing as a tire carrier. Also, the same year, he became a reality television star.
