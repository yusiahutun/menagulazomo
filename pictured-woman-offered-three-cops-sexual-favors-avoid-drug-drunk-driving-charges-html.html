Florida woman offered 'three cops sexual favors to avoid' charges

Publish date: 2024-04-28

A young Florida woman now faces additional charges following her DUI and drug possession arrest after she allegedly offered to perform sex acts on police officers in exchange for letting her free.

Arielle Engert, 24, was pulled over when police saw her car swerving in Clearwater around 2 a.m. Tuesday. Police say she was also in possession of marijuana.

After being transported to Pinellas County jail, Engert is accused of telling Deputy Brian Sudbrink that she would perform oral sex and other gratuitous acts on him if he'd let her off the hook, reports The Smoking Gun.

Arielle Engert is accused of offering Pinellas County cops sexual favors in exchange for setting her free

Police stopped Engert early Tuesday when they say they saw her swerving as she drove on a Clearwater street

Police say they found marijuana in her purse and charged her with misdemeanor DUI and drug possession

Sudbrink declined, but that didn't stop Engert from trying her gambit one more time.

After a body search revealed a packet of cocaine in her bra, Engert was faced with felony charges for narcotics possession and introduction of contraband into a detention facility.

Quick-thinking Engert then offered two other officers--Obed Munoz and Eric Biddle--the same deal as Sudbrink, police say.

They also declined.

Now Engert's initial misdemeanors have snowballed into three separate bribery counts in addition to the earlier felonies.

Engert managed to make her $5,000 bail and was released.

According to TSG, Engert's now gutted Facebook once listed her as a student of philosophy at the University of Florida.
