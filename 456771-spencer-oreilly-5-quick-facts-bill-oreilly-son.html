2. He was involved in a custody case that took three years

After divorcing in 2011, Bill O’Reilly filed for sole custody of his children. The case started around 2012 and dragged on for almost four years, ending in February 2016. Finally, O’Reilly and his ex-wife petitioned a New York divorce court for sole custody.

In 2015, the Nassau County Supreme Court decided the case and granted sole custody of Spencer and his sister to McPhilmy, their mother. However, Bill O’Reilly appealed the ruling, allowing him to maintain shared residential custody of the children while the appeal ran its course.

In February 2016, the appeals court upheld the lower court’s ruling and granted primary custody of the children to McPhilmy.
