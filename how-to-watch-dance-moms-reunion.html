Dance Moms, a reality TV series that aired on Lifetime Network from 2011 to 2019, followed the careers of young dancers and their mothers as they trained and competed in dance competitions across the United States. The series was centered around Abby Lee Dance Company in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, and the strict coaching methods of owner and dance instructor Abby Lee Miller. Throughout its run, Dance Moms gained a cult following for its behind-the-scenes look at the highly competitive nature of the dance industry, often highlighting the intense training sessions, rehearsals, and drama between the dancers’ mothers and occasionally with Miller herself.

    Where is Abby Lee Miller now?

    Abby Lee Miller has had a tumultuous journey since leaving the show in 2020. The former Dance Moms star was indicted in 2015 for fraud-related charges and ultimately pled guilty to bankruptcy fraud and bringing unreported money into the United States. She was sentenced to a year and a day in prison, with her sentence beginning in July 2017. She served approximately eight months at a federal prison in Victorville, California, before being transferred to a halfway house in Long Beach, California, for the remainder of her sentence. By May 2018, Miller had been released early for good behavior.

    However, things didn’t get easier for the former reality star. In April 2018, shortly after transitioning to the halfway house, Miller was diagnosed with Burkitt lymphoma, a rare and aggressive form of non-Hodgkin lymphoma. Her diagnosis was discovered after she underwent spinal surgery for an infection. Following her diagnosis, she underwent multiple surgeries and extensive chemotherapy treatment​, putting her in remission in September 2018. Her recovery included significant physical rehabilitation. As of 2024, Miller continues to face mobility challenges, relying on a wheelchair and mobility aids to get around.
