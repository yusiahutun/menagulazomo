Gangster, 18, who shot dead 16-year-old high school student when he opened fire on rivals is jailed for 155 years

A gang member who opened fire on a crowd after a high school football match in Long Beach, California, has been sentenced to 155 years and six months in prison.

Tom Love Vinson, killed 16-year-old Melody Ross as he was attempting to shoot two men near her in October 2009.

The teenager was outside the entrance to Wilson High School campus when Vinson opened fire with a 2.5 caliber shot gun, hitting her with a single shot. 

Verdict: Tom Love Vinson (left) was sentenced to 155 years and six months in prison for opening fire on a high school crowd

Police said the track athlete was an innocent bystander and Vinson had been targeting two members of a rival gang, who both survived their wounds.

A former Wilson student, Vinson testified during his trial that his targets, aged 18 and 20, were members of a rival gang.

He also stated that he acted in self-defence because one of the men pulled out a gun before he started shooting.   

‘All I seen was a black gun,’ he said.   

Killed: Innocent bystander Melody Ross was outside the entrance to Wilson High School campus when Vinson opened fire with a 2.5 caliber shot gun, killing her with a single shot

Memories: The victim's family spoke of their loss and said they cry every time they think of her

Vinson, who 16 at the time of the shooting, was tried as an adult and apologised to his victim’s family before the judge announced the sentence. 

ABClocal.com reported Vinson said:

"I know apology is nothing compared to a loss of a friend or a family member, but I know how it feels. I never went to that game to hurt nobody. I went to that game to have fun like everybody else. Due to the life I was living it seemed like you can't have fun without there always being conflict.'

The website also reported that Ross' father spoke in court saying that he and her family will never get over the death of their daughter.

‘Every day I live in pain and hopelessness,’ said Vanarath Ross.

‘We have to give up some of the memories of our Melody to cope with her loss. We cry every time we remember or to see something that reminds us of her. We feel like taking our own life in order for us to see Melody again.’

Co-defendant: Daivion Davis was already sentenced to eight years in a juvenile facility

Vinson was sentenced to 50 years to life on the first-degree murder charge for killing Melody Ross and two terms of 40 years to life for the attempted murder of his intended targets.

He was also sentenced for attempted voluntary manslaughter involving a girl who was sitting next to Ross but was not wounded.

Deputy District Attorney Dean Bengston explained that the sentence was appropriate for someone who ‘shoots into a crowd of 100 people, not really caring who they hit.’

He also said he wished that Vinson had shown the remorse he demonstrated in court two years ago.  

Co-defendant Daivion Davis was already sentenced to eight years in a juvenile facility.
