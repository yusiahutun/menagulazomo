United Talent Agency Mints A Host Of New Agents, Executives, And Associates

Publish date: 2024-04-20

From left to right, line one: Eni Akintade, Jordan Gorelick, Olivia Fanaro, A.J. Leone, Riley Folsom and Faith Brown From left to right, line two: Tina Dunca, Connor Morgan, Aïcha Forbes-Diaby, Mallory Guzzi, Alejandra Reyes and Pranav Mandavia From left to right, line three: Angelica Ayala-Torres, Zuzanna Ciolek, Kendall Schreiberg, Liam McNamara and Talia Myers (Courtesy of UTA)

LOS ANGELES & NEW YORK (CelebrityAccess) — United Talent Agency on Friday announced a large slate of promotions across multiple offices and 20 of the talent agency’s divisions.

New agents at UTA include ten new agents in the Los Angeles headquarters and one new agent in UTA’s New York office.

In Los Angeles, the new agents include Pranav Mandavia (Digital Talent); A.J. Leone (Emerging Platforms); Olivia Fanaro (Media Rights); Eni Akintade (Motion Picture/Lit); Jordan Gorlack (Talent); Riley Folsom (Talent/Music Crossover); and Faith Brown, Connor Morgan, Talia Myers and Alejandra Reyes (Television Lit).

In the New York office, Tina Dunca was named an agent focused on music brand partnerships.

Other new promotions in the Los Angeles office include Zuzanna Ciolek (Exec in Fine Arts); Aïcha Forbes-Diaby (Associate in Music Business Affairs); Angelica Ayala-Torres (Exec. Music Innovation); Liam McNamara (Associate in Television Business Affairs); and Kendall Schreiberg (Executive in UTA Marketing.)

In the New York office, Mallory Guzzi was named Designer in Brand Studio.

“This next generation of leaders have exhibited a profound spirit of resilience, innovation and ability that no pandemic could impact,” said UTA Co-President David Kramer. “The promotions serve as important milestones for our colleagues in their careers, as well as a testament to UTA’s continued evolution and growth across multiple business areas. I’m confident our clients and company will be well-served by their talents for years to come.”

The promotions follow the appointment last week of Samantha Kirby Yoh to a leadership role, joining David Zedick in overseeing the company’ global music division. Kirby Yoh most recently lead the music division at rival WME’s New York office and reps a roster that includes Björk, LCD Soundsystem, FKA Twigs, Alicia Keys, and Florence + The Machine among others.
