Teachers Service commission promotions Kenya

Publish date: 2024-08-13

As of November of 2017, the Teachers Service Commission issued new guidelines that would be used in the promotion of teachers. The new Teachers Service Commission promotions guidelines were introduced so as to bring in a competitive recruitment process for the various administrative positions. Promotion of teachers will now be based on three distinct factors. We tell you about the factors and about the new TSC promotion guidelines.

Promotion of teachers will be based on the following factors. First there should be a vacancy in the position the teacher wants to be promoted to. The second requirement is that the teacher should have the required minimum qualification per grade and should have undertaken the professional development courses. Finally, for one to be promoted, they should have the required experience and should have displayed satisfactory performance. What are the new guidelines on TSC online promotions?

Overview of TSC promotion guidelines

The TSC promotion guidelines were effective as of the 8th of November 2017. These promotion guidelines affect teachers who are under the following schemes; scheme of service for graduate teachers, scheme of service for non-graduate teachers and scheme of service for technical teachers and lecturers.

The new TSC career and promotion guidelines are aimed at standardizing the development of teachers in their profession and grading structures by providing new policies and procedures. The grading structure also takes into account the job evaluation report of the teachers who wish to be promoted. The teachers are also required to abide by the code of regulations for them to be able to gain promotion. This is through taking of prescribed programmes that aim to develop them professionally.

The guidelines do not affect all teachers. They affect only the teachers working in the various public institutions including; primary schools and also secondary schools. The guidelines will also apply to the teachers who are teaching in the higher learning institutions including the Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) institutions, Teacher Training Colleges (TTCs). They also affect the teachers who teach in other government institutions like the Kenya Institute of Special Education, (KISE) and the Centre for Mathematics, Science and Technology Education in Africa (CEMASTEA)

Qualifications for teachers under the new guidelines

Before you go and get the TSC promotion form, there is some information on qualification you should know about. First you have to note that TSC promotions will only apply to teachers who have graduated from the various, colleges accredited institutions or those who have passes for the examinations set by the various examination bodies with government approval. Teachers who have studied in the various foreign institutions would have to make sure their certificates are equated by the relevant bodies set to undertake the process.

Here is a list of qualifications for people who want to be on TSC promotion list.

1. Primary school teachers

For a primary school teacher, the minimal requirement was set at a C(plain) in the KCSE exam. They would also need to have a Primary Teacher Education (PTE) Certificate. The entry grades for the disabled teachers(those with visual and also sight impediments) was set at a C- (minus) and they would also need a PTE certificate.

2. Secondary school teachers

For TSC Kenya promotions job group m, the minimal requirement for the KCSE exam was set at grade C+ (plus) and a Diploma in Education. The other combination would be a minimum requirement of KCSE grade C+ (plus) and a Bachelor’s degree in Education or a Postgraduate Diploma in Education.

3. Guidelines for technical college

For one to qualify as lecturer in one a colleges, you will need to have a C+ (plus) in KCSE and a diploma in the technical subject you wish to teach. You will also need to have a Diploma in Technical Education. Apart from that, you can also have a KCSE C- (minus) a certificate, a diploma and Bachelor’s degree in a relevant subject area. You will also be required to have a Diploma in Technical Education.

4. Special needs education teachers

The teacher will be required to have Diploma in Special Needs Education after attaining a grade of C+(plus) in KCSE or alternatively Bachelor’s degree in Special Needs Educations with C+(plus) being the minimum requirement for KCSE exam.

TSC job group grading structure

The Teachers Service Commission also introduced new grading structures to be implemented with the new promotion guidelines. The grades were established for the various types of institutions. The primary school, TVET institutions and KISE teachers would gave eight grades, secondary school teachers have ten grades, six grades for teachers at Centre for Mathematics, Science Technology Education in Africa (CEMASTEA), seven grades for teachers in TTCs.

Which are these grades? Let us take a look at the grades as given by the TSC. For a Primary Teacher I the grade will be T-Scale 6 and a Primary Teacher II the grade will be T-Scale 5

For the TSC job group m promotions, high school teacher III grade will be the same as a Lecturer III and it is T-Scale 6 and high school Teacher II grade will be the same as a Lecturer II- and it will be T-Scale 7. For Diploma holders and high school teacher II as well as Lecturer II it will be T-Scale 7. For Secondary Teacher I as well Lecturer I will be T-scale 8 for holders of Bachelor of Education or its equivalent.

Primary Teacher I-T Scale 6 will be a promotion grade for primary school teachers.

To qualify for promotion to the grade of Primary Teacher I, a teacher will be required to have served as Primary Teacher II-T Scale 5 for a minimum period of three years and must have satisfactory rating in the performance appraisal process.

TSC latest news on promotion of teachers

In February of 2018, the Teachers Service Commission announced that the promotion of teachers would now be more performance oriented. The chief executive of KNUT , Nancy Macharia, told parliament that the new promotions would be based on the work done by the teacher and the output and not on mere academic qualification.
