Kate Middleton, King Charles Allegedly Involved in Conversations About Archie's Skin Tone

Publish date: 2024-03-06

King Charles and Princess Catherine were identified by the British press as the royals named in the initial Dutch publication of Omid Scobie’s book Endgame: Inside the Royal Family and the Monarchy’s Fight for Survival who allegedly participated in conversations about the potential skin tone of Prince Harry and Duchess Meghan’s son, Prince Archie Mountbatten-Windsor.

During a sit-down interview with Oprah Winfrey in 2021, Meghan revealed that members of the royal family had several conversations and concerns about “how dark” her firstborn son’s skin would be, before Archie’s birth. She said this information was relayed to her by Prince Harry. When Winfrey asked who made the statements, Meghan declined to reveal their identities, saying it would be “very damaging” to their reputations.

Scobie said he had identified the royals who discussed Archie’s skin tone, but did not include their names in his book because U.K. libel laws prevented it, according to Good Morning America. However, two passages in the original Dutch translation of Endgame cited Charles and Kate as allegedly involved. The book’s Dutch publisher, Xander Uitgevers, pulled those copies from shelves and told ABC News, “An error occurred in the Dutch translation and is currently being rectified.”

Scobie told ABC News the error is being investigated. “It is of course extremely frustrating and disappointing that the Dutch edition of Endgame contained references to names not included in any other edition of the book, nor the manuscript written, edited, and signed off on by myself,” the author said. “There is now an investigation going on into how this transpired, and I look forward to learning more.”

Royal family members have not publicly commented on Endgame, and a Buckingham Palace spokesperson told ABC News they are “considering all options” regarding King Charles and Princess Kate being named in the controversy.
