The annual Cinco de Mayo celebration, which takes place on May 5, honours the date of Mexico's triumph against the Second French Empire at the Battle of Puebla in 1862 under the command of General Ignacio Zaragoza. How do you celebrate Cinco de Mayo? You can mark the occasion using Mexican folk dancing, mariachi music, parades, Cinco de Mayo quotes, parties, and cooking traditional dishes, including tacos and mole poblano. Although Cinco de Mayo isn't a big thing in Mexico, it has grown immensely popular in the US as a day to honour and celebrate Mexican-American culture and heritage.

  • Feliz Cinco de Mayo - The most common greeting is "Happy Cinco de Mayo"
  • Viva México - This phrase means "Long live Mexico" and is often used to show patriotism and pride for Mexican culture.
  • Salud - This toast means "To your health" and is often said when raising a tequila or other alcoholic beverage.
  • Buen provecho - This phrase means "Enjoy your meal" and is often said before eating.
  • Que viva la fiesta - This means "Long live the party" and is often said when the celebration is in full swing.
  • Arriba, abajo, al centro, adentro! - This chant means "Up, down, to the centre, inside" and is often said before taking a shot of tequila.
  • Olé- This Spanish exclamation expresses joy or excitement and is often used during flamenco dancing or bullfighting.
