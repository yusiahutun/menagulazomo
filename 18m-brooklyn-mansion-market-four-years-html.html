$18m Brooklyn mansion still on the market after four years

Publish date: 2024-05-11

A mansion built by a mob associate and owned by a Russian heiress was once the most expensive home in Brooklyn.

The extravagant property that looks like it belongs in Miami still hasn't been bought, even $12million cheaper and four years after it first went on the market.

2458 National Drive in the Mill Basin neighborhood of Brooklyn, first went on the market in 2013 for $30million.

When no one bought eight-bedroom, nine-bathroom home, the price was taken down to $17million in 2014, but without the guest house. By 2016, it was priced at $12.5million.

Now it's back on the market at $18million, including the adjoining guest home.

2458 National Drive (pictured) in the Mill Basin neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York, is on the market for $18million

The main house was built in by John Rosatti, a multimillionaire and an associate of the Colombo crime family

Rosatti was known as an 'earner' who brought in millions of dollars for the crime family and he was reportedly the wealthiest mob associate in New York City

When he got into some legal trouble, Rosatti sold the house for $3million in the late 1990s

The main house on the gated waterfront property was built by John Rosatti, a multimillionaire and an associate of the Colombo crime family, according to The Village Voice. 

The outlet reported he was the wealthiest mob associate in New York, known as an 'earner' who brought in millions of dollars through his car dealerships. 

In the 1990s, he sold the property for $3million after he had gotten into some legal trouble in the late 1980s. 

He had extended some of his property into the water by filling in the tidal wetlands with landfill, which violated New York's Tidal Wetlands Act, the Voice reported.

At the time, officials said his actions caused the destruction of 2,000 square feet of Mill Basin wetlands. Rosatti got out of those legal troubles with only a low, six-figure fine.

Galina Anisimova, a Russian heiress bought the property from Rosatti. Her daughter Anna Anisimova is known as the 'Russian Paris Hilton'.

The family improved the property over the course of the next fifteen years, helped by designer Noel Jeffrey, according to Top Ten Real Estate Deals. 

The Anisimova family still owns the property, though they have been trying to sell it since 2013.

The main house is 14,000 square feet with four stories. It has four water-view master suites, eight full spa baths and 5,500 square feet of outdoor space on multiple terraces.

The property also includes a 7,800 square feet guest house, a 1,000 square-foot swimming pool and spa, an outdoor pavilion that seats 40, an outdoor kitchen and a multi-slip marina with 257 feet of waterfront on Jamaica Bay.
