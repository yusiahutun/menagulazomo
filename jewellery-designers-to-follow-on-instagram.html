20 jewellery designers to follow on Instagram

Publish date: 2024-04-23

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

1

Nadia Shelbaya

MORE

Nadia Shelbaya is well-loved in the Copenhagen jewellery scene for her understated pieces, which combine her Egyptian and Danish heritage. Shelbaya's love for jewellery dates back to her childhood, where she spent her summers in Cairo and fell in love with the country's ornate jewels. Her collections are created from tactile gold and silver that's melted together with Baroque pearls.

2

Jessie Thomas

MORE

Jessie Thomas, a London-based goldsmith and designer, learnt her craft from her father and celebrated goldsmith, David Thomas (his pieces are held in permanent collections of institutions including the Victoria & Albert Museum and the Goldsmiths Company). Subtlety and wearability are key in the brand's aesthetic, with every piece being the perfect balance of statement and simplicity, meaning you'll love your piece forever despite passing trends.

3

Anissa Kermiche

MORE

Anissa Kermiche not only inspires our jewellery box but our homes, too. The brand behind the infamous boob jugs and butt vases, the designer's jewellery is also worthy of note. More like mini works of art, Kermiche celebrates the female form throughout her brand and her jewellery is no different.

4

Missoma

MORE

Born as a collaboration between the designer Marisa Hordern, her sister and her mother, Missoma is known for brightly coloured pieces and contemporary designs. The affordable price points mean that you feel no qualms about shopping as you stalk on Instagram.

5

La Manso

MORE

Barcelona-based La Manso has quickly become a hit with not only fashion lovers but the celebrity world, too. The likes of Bella Hadid, Dua Lipa and Miley Cyrus have all been spotted wearing La Manso's playful designs that boast Nineties nostalgia. Founder Adriana Manso started the brand just three years ago, after taking inspiration from her grandmother's collection of glamorous acrylic jewellery.

6

Sandra Alexandra

MORE

Founded between designer Sandra's London home studio and her motherland Spain, Sandra Alexandra was born. Her designs are instantly recognisable, down to her delicately handmade designed pieces that use natural materials such as glass, metal and pearls that transform into fruits, vegetables and delicate glass beads.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

Atelier Romy

MORE

Created by women for women, Atelier Romy recognises individual style. The brand is focused online to cut out the middleman and offers gold and silver pieces at more affordable prices. With over 20 years of experience in the jewellery industry, you can see why Atelier Romy is so popular with the most stylish women.

8

Mociun

MORE

Pronounced mō-shen, this fine jewellery brand has a homegrown feel as it's designed by founder Caitlin Mociun and hand made in New York. The line is known for unique turquoise and diamond combinations, and one-of-kind stone cluster rings, using antique diamonds and rare gemstones.

9

Vanda Jacintho

MORE

Vanda Jacintho, a Brazilian designer, has the most beautiful pieces. Within her jewellery line, she works with sustainable materials like mahogany wood and handcrafted resin. Her sources of inspiration stem from childhood memories of growing up in Brazil on the family farm in Pantanal, to her leading inspirations from the world of arts such as Sonia Delaunay and Escher.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

Anita Berisha

MORE

Anita Berisha's Instagram bio explains her brand perfectly as "wearable art". Founded in 2016 by self-taught designer Anita Berisha in New York, the label is known for its playful take on jewellery through experimenting with different shapes and textures. Every collection has its own story as pieces are recycled and can stand the test of time.

11

Monica Vinader

MORE

Bridging the gap between fine and fashion jewellery, Monica Vinader's passion for colour dictates her collections. Her jewellery can be stacked, personalised and worn every day - look to her Instagram account for inspiration on how to style it.

12

Sophie Bille Brahe

MORE

The Copenhagen-based Sophie Bille Brahe already has a dedicated following. Follow for beautifully pure images of her designs. We're particularly enamoured with her delicate pearl pieces - the perfect finishing touch to any outfit, no matter the occasion.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

13

Arctic Fox & Co

MORE

Inspired by all things Icelandic and Scandinavian, Arctic Fox & Co has the most gorgeous pieces that are a welcome addition to any jewellery box. The label has sustainability at its heart and truly cares about the environment around us, donating five per cent of all proceeds to WWF.

14

LeiVanKash

MORE

Influenced largely by her Persian heritage, Leila Kashanipour creates jewellery that reveals intricate craftsmanship while being playful and innovative. Follow Kashanipour for an insight into her jet-set lifestyle with outfit inspiration from her covetable wardrobe.

15

Delfina Delettrez

MORE

Delfina Delettrez's jewellery offers a unique and surreal aesthetic that has attracted a cult following. The designer takes inspiration from the human body, nature and childhood memories to create the delicately handcrafted pieces, using organic materials and precious stones.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

16

Repossi

MORE

At a young age, Gai Repossi took the helm at her family's jewellery house, originally founded by her grandfather in 1920. The epitome of French chic, and influenced by her degree in archaeology, her jewellery is characterised by modern minimalism that is reflected in the brand's Instagram account.

17

Annoushka

MORE

Established in 2009 by Annoushka Ducas, who previously founded Links of London, this label seeks to cater to the modern woman's desire for jewellery to be part of her personal identity. Follow for stunning shots of the latest designs and updates on artist collaborations.

18

Georg Jensen

MORE

With a history that spans more than 100 years, Georg Jensen continues to make jewellery that feels contemporary. The brand's Instagram page reveals the extent of the Georg Jensen world - featuring everything from watches to silverware and gifts.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

19

Astley Clarke

MORE

Astley Clarke is a brilliant store for precious pieces that you can wear every day, stocking a number of brands in addition to its own label. Follow for updates on the latest designs.

20

Nadine Ghosn

MORE

If you're into kitsch and quirky jewellery, then you need to follow Nadine Ghosn. The designer became an overnight success after quitting her job and starting her own business from scratch, creating fun and inventive pieces inspired by the world around her. The brand's signature burger ring has taken the jewellery world by storm, which comes as no surprise considering how Instagram-worthy it is.
