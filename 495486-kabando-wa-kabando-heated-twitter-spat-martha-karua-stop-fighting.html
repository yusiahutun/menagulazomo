Kabando wa Kabando In Heated Twitter Spat With Martha Karua: &quot;Stop Fighting Me&quot;

Publish date: 2024-02-15
  • Former Mukurweini MP Kabando wa Kabando accused Narc Kenya leader Martha Karua of not being a team player
  • On her part, Karua said Kabando failed to understand how her party operates, insisting that Narc Kenya party was democratic
  • Since Kabando resigned from Narc Kenya, he has been exchanging harsh words with his former party leader of social media

Narc Kenya leader Martha Karua and former Mukurweini MP Kabando wa Kabando have escalated their political fallout.

In a tweet, Kabando claimed that the lack of basic party processes made him quit Narc Kenya and Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition Party.

Kabando on why he quit Narc Kenya

"Prevailing status and lack of basic party processes caused me to quit. It is a decent thing to do in the face of unyielding circumstances rather than rock boat," Kabando said on Wednesday, February 22.

Karua would, however, hit back at the former Mukurweini lawmaker, accusing him of not understanding the party's working.

According to Karua, Narc Kenya was well-run and was not under the control of any individual, as claimed by Kabando.

"You never took time to know the party. Narc Kenya is well-run and institutionalised. The party leader is not the party," Karua said.

In a quick rejoinder, Kabando wondered why Karua was the only party official answering his queries.

"If the party is well run, you wouldn't be the only one answering such simple queries; If the party is institutionalised, other party officials would also be visible. I explained myself in detail via private mail to you, in which case I'd rather leave it there. Stop fighting me. You're a patriot of integrity, but not team player," Kabando stated.

Kabando claims Ruto, Raila in secret talks

In another development, the former Mukurweini MP made allegations that are set to ruffle feathers within the corridors of power.

The former lawmaker claimed there was a conspiracy among the top leaders in Kenya to hoodwink Kenyans.

Kabando claimed there have been high-level talks between Ruto and Azimio La Umoja One Kenya leader.

Kabando stated that the ODM leader was on his way to power in parliament.

"Making this observation without fear, favour, bias, or prejudice: Ruto is baiting Azimio while Raila is on his way to Parliament via Ruto's sly constitutional amendments. Ruto and Raila in plot; secret talks. High voltage palace conspiracy against gullible, suffering Kenyans," Kabando wrote on Monday, February 20.
