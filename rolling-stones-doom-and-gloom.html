Hear the new Rolling Stones single – Doom and Gloom

The Stones debuted a new track on Radio 2 this morning. Here's your chance for a repeat listen

Reading on mobile? Listen here.

The Stones are back – and they're rocking surprisingly hard. Doom and Gloom, one of two new songs on their forthcoming greatest hits compilation, Grrr!, due on November 12. Producer Don Was has emphasised the heavy side of the band, while sprucing up the rhythm section behind the guitars of Keith Richards and Ronnie Wood.

The song was played twice by Radio 2's Chris Evans at 8.15am this morning, and now we can post it for your delectation. Let us know what you think in the comments thread below.

Although no tour dates have officially been announced for the band's 50th anniversary, Richards rather let the cat out of the bag by saying the group would be playing in London and New York. Now longtime sideman Bobby Keys has gone further. The sax player told Billboard: "[The Rolling Stones are] gonna do some more concerts, starting in November with two in England and then a couple here in the States, then there's a few added concerts after that."
