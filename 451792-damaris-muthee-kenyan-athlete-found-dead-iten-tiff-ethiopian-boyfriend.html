Damaris Muthee: Kenyan Athlete Found Dead in Iten after Tiff with Ethiopian Boyfriend

Publish date: 2024-02-09
  • Damaris Muthee is said to have met her death while in the company of her Ethiopian boyfriend, identified as Koki Fai
  • According to sources, the two lovebirds were not on good terms, the Ethiopian luring the deceased into the death trap before fleeing the country
  • He called while outside the country to inform the deceased's family that their kin had committed suicide, further directing them to where the body was

A lifeless body of a female athlete has been found in a house in Iten, Elgeyo Marakwet county.

Lured into death trap

24-year-old Damaris Muthee Mutua was allegedly lured to the death trap by her Ethiopian lover, a fellow athlete identified as Koki Fai.

A close source intimated to this writer that Damaris and her boyfriend were on a rough phase in their relationship, which is believed to have led to her untimely demise.

Koki reportedly fled from the country after the incident, which police suspected ensued on Sunday, April 17.

He later called the deceased's kin to inform them that she had committed suicide, further revealing to them where the body was lying.

"The boyfriend called her and she acceded to her advances. He killed her and immediately left the country. He used a different number to call the deceased's kin informing them of where the body was lying. The body has not been removed from the scene," said the source.

Police were informed before making it to the scene, which is currently sealed off as investigations into the circumstances around the murder begin.

Damaris, a Kenyan by birth, had acquired the citizenship of Bahrain, where her career was on a trajectory to its pinnacle.

This came months after another promising athlete died at the hands of her boyfriend.

Agness Tirop was found dead on Wednesday, October 13, with grave stab wounds on her neck and abdomen in her house in Iten, Elgeyo Marakwet county.

Her husband Rotich, 41, was arrested the following day in the coastal city of Mombasa while trying to flee the country.

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations said Rotich had rammed his getaway vehicle into a lorry at Mlolongo, along Mombasa Road, as he tried to escape arrest.
