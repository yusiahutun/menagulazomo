'Miss January' has 'no regrets' over Australia Women's team's nude calendar

Publish date: 2024-07-21

A former footballer who posed completely naked for a team calendar insists she has no regrets over the stunt.

Twenty-four years ago, the female game was in a very different place to where it is now, with the biggest-ever Women’s World Cup set to kick off in Australia. While many of the teams competing Down Under are made up of full-time professionals, that wasn’t the case at the turn of the century.

The Australian national team was desperately struggling for both attention and funding. With the Olympic Games – where women’s football is a big deal – in Sydney looming, it was vital they took the opportunity to gain the maximum possible exposure.

So they took the drastic step of exposing themselves, quite literally, by producing a nude calendar. There were no balls delicately placed over private areas, they bared all and thrust a new spotlight on the team which brought them to the public’s attention.

Reflecting on the calendar, Tracie McGovern, who was Miss January, has absolutely no regrets over the stunt, which nowadays would attract huge controversy and, in some cases, outrage.

“I don't regret it at all. I look at that photo after 20 years and I'm proud of it,” McGovern, now 45, told the BBC.

“We didn't have the funding and the infrastructure around us that they do today. It was mainly about the profile. We wanted to get bums on seats at the Games, but I don't think any of us expected the attention that we got."

Not every member of the squad took part and McGovern admitted there were a few awkward moments during the shoot.

"I had a great time, although the team shot was a little awkward," she said. "We all had our robes on, we got into position and then we threw the robes off, had the photo taken and then scrambled to get them back on.

"There were some feminists that felt that we had sold out. I totally respect that opinion and not all of the team took part in the calendar photoshoot. I did it for my own personal reasons. And then I did it for the greater good of the team. I think we achieved that objective.

"It was controversial, but again we got people to go to our games and we got the name out there. Could we have done it any other way? I'm not sure. I really hope that people will start to embrace that part of history and don't feel shameful about it."

McGovern, who is part of the hosts’ delegation at the tournament, was asked what her family think about the calendar.

She laughed: "My kids just say 'oh no, not your bum again mum'."
