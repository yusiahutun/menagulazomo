The 10 best Tumblr alternatives for picture blogs in 2021

Publish date: 2024-02-28

Tumblr is one of the best micro-blogging sites for photographers and a mix of Twitter, Facebook, and WordPress. As a user, you can post text, videos, images, audio, and links. If you follow other blogs, you will see their content on your feed. But as impressive as the platform is, you may find that you need something different. Many Tumblr alternatives out there allow you to own a blog and put your message across. However, only a few of them come near the unique qualities of Tumblr.

Tumblr is one of the best platforms for photography inspiration. Photographers use the website to see what others are doing and copy their styles and showcase their talents. Furthermore, the site provides you with free hosting, and you can choose from hundreds of beautiful themes to customise the design.

10 best Tumblr alternatives

Tumblr is not the only website for picture blogs. Many Tumblr alternatives allow you to upload images or create an online portfolio of your work. Here are ten websites worth considering:

1. Blogger

Blogger is one of the best websites like Tumblr. It allows you to set up a new blog and get it running in less than ten minutes. Like Tumblr, you do not require any programming skills to start your image blog. The site lets you store thousands of photos for free.

You can use it to create a picture blog that fits your style by selecting thousands of templates with flexible layouts or design something from scratch. The site gives you a free domain to host your blog. An advantage of using the site is it has an in-built analytics feature that lets you see where your audience is coming from and what they like.

2. Ello

Ello is the best alternative to Tumblr and an ideal platform for photographers and artists. It is a website that creatives use to showcase their art, photography, fashion, and web culture. A unique aspect of the site is it focuses on creators and creativity. Unlike Tumblr and other passive social networks, Ello provides connections and opportunities to its users.

As a new user, you will get your profile page. Anybody can access it and start viewing your pictures or creations. Also, you can choose which accounts to follow and which ones to see your profile. Although new users can join the site, they can only participate through an invite.

3. DeviantArt

DeviantArt is one of the best online resources for photographers and artists. It is a Tumblr replacement site that allows users to share their photography skills and receive feedback on their creations. The platform also lets users upload, categorise, and promote their photos through groups or journals.

Groups and journals are similar to blog entries. Other friends or followers can view images uploaded by an artist or photographer. Also, journals allow users to share their thoughts, experiences, and tales behind the art. Although DeviantArt is free, some of its features are available at a premium price.

4. Flickr

Flickr is an excellent photo-sharing tool and the web home of many camera wielders globally. Millions of people upload pictures on the platform daily on every imaginable subject. The site allows you to keep a blog of moments captured on your camera or showcase off the best photos you have found elsewhere.

If you are a budding photographer, Flickr is a place to share your photos with friends, family, and categorised communities freely, privately, and securely. Flickr may be the best Tumblr alternative because the platform is not for professional photographers only. It attracts amateur photographers too, and one can showcase their creativity.

5. Imgur

Imgur is an image-sharing community and one of the best image hosting platforms similar to Tumblr. The site can host your still and animated images. It then allows others users to vote or comment on your submissions. You can also organise your pictures easily by creating folders.

One thing that makes Imgur one of the best apps like Tumblr is it allows you to post all types of images. You can upload professional photographs, memes, GIFs, and other inspiring creations. Imgur has many features you may find helpful. For example, it offers private uploads, and you can embed a picture immediately after you have uploaded it.

6. Medium

Medium is the best comprehensive traditional blogging platform today. It is a platform where readers can read classic blog-style posts and where users can share their thoughts. The website also allows users to add images to convey a story. You can drag and drop single or multiple images into your post and publish it for everyone to see.

Medium is also one of the best websites similar to Tumblr for writers. You can share your stories with the world using an in-built editor. Publishing on the platform is free. After you post your thoughts, Medium will distribute the content to your followers and other readers that follow relevant topics.

7. SmugMug

SmugMug is a platform that allows you to store, access, and share your pictures. It is a site designed for casual point-and-shooters, phone photographers, and full-time photography professionals. You can use it to display your photos and secure them in the cloud with unlimited storage.

SmugMug is one of the best sites like Tumblr for pictures that give you a clean layout and optimal settings. It allows you to load your images quickly and displays them beautifully. That makes it an excellent website builder for people that want to show off or sell their visual work online. The site offers both free and premium plans for new users.

8. Stampsy

Stampsy is a platform for sharing, curating, and discovering visual content that tells a story. You can use it to add your creations to relevant collections curated by other users. Alternatively, you can start your collection and use it whenever you like, including inviting others to view your images.

As a user, you can follow a collection and view all stamps on your feed. You will also discover outstanding publications from top creators or inspirational stories from your friends. Stampsy also allows you to create photo stories, mood boards, and visual essays and share them with its ever-growing community.

9. Typepad

Typepad is a reliable and flexible blogging platform that gives users control over what they want to publish. The site puts you in control of your site design, webpage, and individual posts. If you are a blogger, Typepad is a website you should consider. It has a simple user interface where you can pen down your thoughts and share your passions with the world.

Typepad is also the best platform for business users. It allows you to monetise your content by choosing the method to maximise viewership. Additionally, the service lets you understand how well your posts are performing. You can use analytics systems built into the service to develop new content and attract new readers.

10. WordPress

WordPress is the best website builder for photographers. Unlike other sites like Tumblr, the platform gives you the creative freedom to customise your blog. You can access unlimited designs and thousands of applications and extensions. It also offers access to tons of functionality and SEO options to ensure better visibility of your picture blog on search engine results pages.

The advantage of using WordPress is you can use it to create anything. You can start with a static website for your pictures or an advanced e-commerce store for selling your professional photographs.

If you are looking for the best Tumblr alternatives for picture blogs, consider platforms like Ello, Blogger, WordPress, Stampsy, and Medium. You can use the websites to create your online photography portfolio to attract clients or share your inspirations. The sites have better administration features, are scalable, and offer robust support.
