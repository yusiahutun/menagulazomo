Faith Kipyegon to Receive KSh 5m, House Worth KSh 6m for Breaking 2 World Records, Ruto Announces

Publish date: 2024-05-23
  • Faith Kipyegon and Ferdinand Omanyala were hosted by President William Ruto at State House, Nairobi
  • The president awarded Kipyegon KSh 5 million and a three-bedroom house worth KSh 6 million for breaking the 1500m and 5000m world records
  • Ruto said going forward any athlete who breaks a world record will be awarded KSh 5 million by the government

Nairobi - President William Ruto has announced a new rewarding system for Kenyan athletes who break world records.

Speaking after hosting the 1500m and 5000m world record holder Faith Kipyegon at State House, Nairobi, Ruto said the government will be awarding KSh 5 million for any world record.

Kipyegon awarded house at Park Road

Consequently, the president announced that the government will award Faith KSh 5 million and a house worth KSh 6 million for breaking two world records.

"Going forward, we have decided as the government of Kenya that any Kenyan who participates in a world event and breaks a world record, unlike in the past where there was no recognition, going forward, for any world record broken by a Kenyan, Kenya is going to award Ksh.5 million.And we are going to start today with Faith she has broken two world records in 1500 meters in Italy and 5,000 meters in France. The government is going to award her a total of Ksh.5 million for one record and we are going to give her a house worth Ksh.6 million for the other record." said Ruto.

Kipyegon breaks 5000m world record

Recently Kipyegon made the country proud after she broke the women's 5,000m world record at the Paris Diamond League on Friday, June 9, barely a week after shattering the 1,500m in Italy.

She clocked 14:05:20, becoming the first Kenyan woman to hold the world record in the 5,000m record.

The previous record holder, Letesenbet Gidey of Ethiopia, was leading the pack, but Kipyegon overtook her with 600m to go.

Kipyegon was unrelenting as the Ethiopian breathed down her neck, and she broke Gidey's previous record of 14:06:62.

Photos at the finish line depicted an emotional Kipyegon, stunned at learning that she had broken the world record. She later admitted that her target was to give her best.

"No, I did not think about the world record or know how I made it. I was so surprised. I just wanted to improve my personal best," said the 29-year-old.

On Friday, June 2, Kipyegon smashed the 1,500m record in Florence, clocking 3:49.11.
