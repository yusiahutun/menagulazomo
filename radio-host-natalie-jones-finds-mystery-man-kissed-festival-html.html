Radio host Natalie Jones finds mystery man she kissed at festival

Publish date: 2024-09-10

A woman embarked on a desperate search for a mystery man she shared a kiss with at a music festival, sparking a large 'online search party'. 

Natalie Jones asked listeners of the radio show she hosts to identify a man she had an intimate moment with while waiting in line for a drink at a festival before they parted ways. 

The search caught the attention of thousands of people and eventually the man, named Cole, was tracked down and reunited with his festival lover.

Natalie is one of the hosts of the Q102 weekday afternoon show in Cincinnati, Ohio and attended a music festival an hour and 20 minutes' drive south in Lexington, Kentucky where she met Cole. 

She and Cole chatted while they were waiting to buy a drink before a friend of Natalie's snapped the pair when they suddenly locked lips. 

Radio host Natalie Jones (right) started an online search party for the mystery man she kissed at a festival. The man, named Cole (left) was tracked down and the pair reunited

After they were separated and the festival finished, Natalie wanted to know who her mystery sweetheart was so posted the image on Facebook attracting a flurry of responses from users who immediately got on the case to find him. 

Internet sleuths managed to track down Cole who later appeared on Natalie's radio show with her co-hosts Roy and Freddy Mac. 

Cole said he thought Natalie was only using him to cut into the bar line. 

'I got that vibe when she moved in at first and I was kinda like whatever, I can be that guy,' Cole admitted. 

'We introduced ourselves, she told me what she did and then I told her what I did, a little bit of conversation about other things, she told me she was buying my drinks and I was like 'ah I don't know'.'

He said Natalie 'insisted' on buying his drinks then they 'turned away from the bar and one thing led to the other.'

Natalie's co-hosts asked why Cole didn't stop to get Natalie's number but he said, 'it all happened so fast' and he was whisked away by his friends. 

He said the kiss was 'definitely a moment' and admitted to looking for Natalie online by seeing if she had left a Google review for the festival. 

Natalie confessed she thought Cole might have been on a bachelor party thanks to his novelty flamingo romper and kissed her in a 'dare situation' but remained intrigued by her festival fling. 

Cole assured Natalie that he was 'absolutely not' in a relationship and the pair agreed to keep talking despite living in towns five hours away from each other. 

'I've never attempted (a long-distance relationship) though we look really good together,' Cole said.
