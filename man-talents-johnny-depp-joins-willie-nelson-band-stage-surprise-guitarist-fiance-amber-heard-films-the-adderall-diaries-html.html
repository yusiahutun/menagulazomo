Before he was an award winning actor he started off his creative career in a band.

Johnny Depp went back to his musical roots as he joined Willie Nelson and his band at the Blue Hills Bank Pavillion in Boston on Tuesday.

The 51-year-old Pirates Of The Carribean actor was a surprise guest at the gig, with country singer Willie simply introducing his famous pal to the stage as 'Johnny'.

Rocking out: Johnny Depp was the surprise guest at Willie Nelson's gig where he played guitar for the whole set

Despite having an illustrious acting career that has seen him win Golden Globe and Screen Actors Guild awards, Johnny started off his career as a musician.

The Sweeney Todd actor even played guitar on the Oasis song 'Fade In-Out' in 1997, playing with the band for two years, he has released several songs and was the lead guitarist of band The Kids in 1980. 

It is not the first time the actor has played alongside the country music legend, the talented actor performed with him at South by Southwest festival in March, and was also introduced incognito as 'John'.
