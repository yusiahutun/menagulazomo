Career Beginnings

Rise to Prominence

Without much previous experience, Travis landed the role of his career when he was selected as the school bully Joey Caruso, in the TV sitcom “Everybody Hates Chris”. The series focused on the childhood and teen years of the comedy star Chris Rock, whose character was played by Tyler James Williams.

Travis featured in 54 episodes, finding new ways to make Chris’ life miserable. Regardless, the role made him very popular, and during his time on the show, he also received offers for other projects. He appeared in the TV series “Nip/Tuck” in 2006, then in 2007 he had a minor role in the romantic comedy film “License to Wed”, starring Mandy Moore, John Krasinski, and Robin Williams.

The following year he made an appearance in the comedy film “Step Brothers”, with Will Ferrell, John C. Reilly, and Mary Steenburgen in the lead roles, and also featured as Billy in the horror film “Mostly Ghostly”, with such stars as Madison Pettis, Sterling Beaumon, and Luke Benward.

Career Decline

After “Everybody Hates Chris” ended, Travis struggled to obtain notable roles, and until 2015, had only a few one-time appearances.
